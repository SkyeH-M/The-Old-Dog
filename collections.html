Come Visit Us

We have an expansive collection of books for you to browse, from non-fiction political and scientific texts to manga, and young adult fiction.
If you aren't sure what your next read should be pop down and a staff member will be happy to recommend something you're sure to love.
Check out our staff picks of the month below, we know there'll be something that you won't be able to put down...

Queer Horror

This month our pick is...


Tell Me I'm Worthless by Alison Rumfitt

"A dark, unflinching haunted house novel that takes readers from the well of the literary gothic, up through Brighton's queer scene, and out into the heart of modern day trans experience in the UK." - Cipher Press

Rumfitt expertly explores themes of queer and trans identities, and the creeping horrors of facism bubbling under the surface of modern day Britain.

Authors of Colour

This month our pick is...


A Little Life by Hanya Yanagihara

"The million copy bestseller, Hanya Yanagihara's A little Life is an immensely powerful and heartbreaking novel of brotherly love and the limits of human endurance." - Pan Macmillan

"A Little Life makes for near-hypnotically compelling reading, a vivid, hyperreal portrait of human existence that demands intense emotional investment." - Independent

Political Texts

This month our pick is...


Fearing The Black Body by Sabrina Strings

"Fearing the Black Body participates in a critical discourse that exposes the convergence of anxieties about race and fatness as it manifests in our current fat phobia. The text successfully demonstrates how the Black body has been subject to ongoing surveillance, and more specifically how it has been co-opted as a site where struggles around race and class issues play out." - Fat Studies
